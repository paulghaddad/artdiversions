A little about Amy

Thank you for visiting my site!

John Lubbock Sr. once said: “Art is unquestionably one of the purest and highest elements in human happiness.” For me, there is a lot of truth to this statement. I created this blog to share my passion: writing about the arts.

I am a graduate student in the Sotheby’s Institute of Art and a writer. I am completing my Master’s thesis by exploring the use of cybernetics in Silicon Valley’s contemporary art during the twenty-first century. I have enjoyed researching and writing about this emerging art market and renowned technology center that is Silicon Valley.

In addition to my studies, I am also a writer. I write articles for this blog on the art world: from art fairs to exhibitions. My articles have examined a variety of artists and their art: Henri Matisse's cut-outs, Marina Abramović’s performances, Afshin Pirhashemi’s Iranian women, and David Hockney’s iPad drawings, among many others. And the articles I write for an online antique company have allowed yet another lens for me to analyze the art world.

The ever-changing art industry is fascinating and exciting. I enjoy using the written word as an opportunity to analyze, think critically and share my passion with others.

Keep up with the changing art world and my journey on this site!
